NeuroSight AI: Parkinson's Prediction

Explainable Parkinson's Disease prediction using vocal acoustics, the custom Feature Performance Index (fPI), and gradient boosted trees.

Python (3.11) XGBoost (1.5.0) Scikit-Learn Pandas & NumPy Jupyter Notebooks

01 // Research Objective & Scope

Parkinson's Disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterized by the gradual loss of dopaminergic neurons. While motor symptoms like tremors and rigidity are commonly used for clinical diagnosis, vocal impairment (dysphonia) is often one of the earliest indicators, appearing years before gross motor dysfunction.

NeuroSight AI is an applied machine learning research project exploring the non-invasive prediction of Parkinson's Disease using vocal acoustic biomarkers. By utilizing a custom-engineered Feature Performance Index (fPI) alongside advanced tree-based classifiers like XGBoost, this project demonstrates how combination features and structured tabular classification can detect early-stage dysphonia with high precision and interpretability.

Note: This repository represents a research-oriented predictive modeling study using voice recording datasets. It is not designed, certified, or intended for clinical medical diagnosis or diagnostic product use.

Research Focus

03 // Feature Performance Index (fPI) Engineering

The raw dataset consists of vocal acoustic measurements from 198 subjects (140 Parkinson's, 58 healthy controls). Features cover pitch frequency, amplitude perturbation (Jitter/Shimmer), noise measures, and fractal dynamics.

To improve the mathematical separation between cohorts, I engineered a domain-specific composite metric called the Feature Performance Index (fPI), combining fractal scaling, chaotic complexity, and variation:

fPI = log10(DFA × D2 × spread2)

DFA (Fractal Scaling)

Detrended Fluctuation Analysis measures the self-similarity and long-range fractal scaling exponents of the voice signal.

D2 (Chaotic Complexity)

Correlation dimension estimates the signal's complexity and characterizes the vocal fold oscillation dynamics.

spread2 (Pitch Variation)

A nonlinear measure quantifying fundamental frequency variations to isolate micro-tremors in vocal control.

06 // Strategic Research Insights

Core Findings:

  • Predictive Signal Strength: Perturbation measures like Pitch Period Entropy (PPE) and amplitude variations (Shimmer) exhibit high predictive signal strength, capturing vocal fold control degradation.
  • Complexity Metrics: Correlation Dimension (D2) and Recurrence Period Density Entropy (RPDE) successfully differentiate healthy voice structures from Parkinsonian dysphonia.
  • Explainability Value: The composite fPI metric scales exponents, chaotic dynamics, and pitch variation into a single composite log-index, acting as a powerful non-invasive acoustic biomarker.
